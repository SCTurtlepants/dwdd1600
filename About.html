Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

There were 56 signers of the Declaration of Independence. Some of the most famous signers include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration; later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence; later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and thinker; helped secure French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; known for his large, bold signature—“John Hancock” became a slang term for a signature.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Leader of the Sons of Liberty; major figure in organizing resistance to British rule; cousin of John Adams.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four major American founding documents (Continental Association, Declaration, Articles of Confederation, and Constitution).
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer; the last surviving signer, died in 1832.
